Here are 5 specific templates for a **Font Pairing Guide**, each designed for a different design context. Each template includes its core components and a step-by-step guide on how to use it.

---

### 1. The "Classic Editorial" Template
**Best for:** Blogs, magazines, long-form articles, and book layouts.

**What it contains:**
- **Header Font:** A serif font (e.g., Playfair Display, Garamond, or Chronicle Text) – used for titles, headings, and pull quotes.
- **Body Font:** A clean, highly readable sans-serif (e.g., Source Sans Pro, Open Sans, or Helvetica Neue).
- **Accent Font:** A geometric sans-serif (e.g., Montserrat, Futura) – used only for sub-headings or numbers.
- **Pairing Rule:** 1 serif + 1 neutral sans-serif + 1 optional accent.
- **Sample CSS/Code Snippet:** Pre-written font-family declarations for web use.
- **Visual Example:** A mockup showing a title in the serif font, a body paragraph in the sans-serif, and a pull quote in a bold weight of the serif.

**How to use it:**
1. Open the template and replace the placeholder font names with your chosen serif and sans-serif.
2. Apply the serif font to all `h1`, `h2`, and blockquote tags.
3. Apply the sans-serif font to all `p`, `li`, and `figcaption` tags.
4. Use the accent font sparingly (e.g., only on `h3` or `.post-number`).
5. Test readability: Ensure the body font has a line-height of 1.6+ and a font-size of 16–18px.
