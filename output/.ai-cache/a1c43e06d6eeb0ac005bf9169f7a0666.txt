Here are 5 specific templates for a **Color Palette Collection**, each designed for a different use case. Each template includes its structure, contents, and step-by-step instructions for use.

---

### 1. The "Brand Identity" Template
**Purpose:** For creating and documenting a brand’s core color system, including primary, secondary, accent, and neutral tones.

**Contents:**
- **Header:** Brand Name, Project/Client, Date.
- **Color Swatches:** 5–7 circles or squares with hex codes (e.g., #2C3E50, #E74C3C).
- **Color Roles:** Labels for each swatch (e.g., Primary, Secondary, Accent, Background, Text).
- **Usage Notes:** A small text box for each color (e.g., "Use for main buttons," "Use for body text").
- **Accessibility Check:** A mini table with contrast ratios (e.g., Primary on White = 4.5:1).
- **Example Application:** A mockup of a logo or button using the palette.

**How to Use:**
1. Fill in the brand name and date.
2. Choose 5–7 colors that represent the brand’s personality (e.g., a tech brand might use blue and gray).
3. Assign each color a role (primary, secondary, etc.) and write a short note on where to apply it.
4. Use a contrast checker (like WebAIM) to fill in the accessibility table.
5. Add a small visual example (e.g., a colored rectangle with text) to show the palette in action.
