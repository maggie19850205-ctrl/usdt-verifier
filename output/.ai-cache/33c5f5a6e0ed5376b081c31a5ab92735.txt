**What It Is & Why It Matters**  
A Color Palette Collection is a curated set of color families (typically 5–15 swatches) that defines the visual identity of a brand, website, or design project. Instead of picking colors on the fly, you build a reusable system that ensures consistency across all assets—from logos and social media graphics to product packaging and UI elements. This saves hours of rework, prevents visual chaos, and communicates professionalism. For example, a travel blog might collect a palette of warm sand (#E8D5B7), ocean teal (#2A9D8F), sunset coral (#F4A261), and deep navy (#264653), then apply these exact hex codes to every image, button, and text block.

**Tools & Step-by-Step Implementation**  
1. **Gather inspiration**: Use **Coolors.co** (free web app) to generate palettes from photos, or **Adobe Color** to extract colors from uploaded images.  
2. **Define your base colors**: Pick 1–3 dominant hues (e.g., primary brand color, accent, neutral). Use **Color Hunt** or **Paletton** to find complementary shades.  
3. **Build the collection**: In **Figma** or **Canva**, create a dedicated “Color Palette” page with swatches labeled by role (e.g., “Primary – #E63946,” “Background – #F1FAEE”).  
4. **Test contrast & accessibility**: Use **WebAIM Contrast Checker** to ensure text-background combos meet WCAG AA standards (4.5:1 ratio for normal text).  
5. **Export & document**: Save your palette as a PDF or shareable link (Coolors offers “Export as Image”), then add hex/RGB/CMYK values to a style guide.
