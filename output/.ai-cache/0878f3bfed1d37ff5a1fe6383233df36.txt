Here is a 10-item actionable checklist for a Brand Identity Template, with each item starting with a verb and being specific and measurable.

1.  **Define** your brand’s core purpose by writing a single, clear mission statement (under 30 words) that explains why the brand exists beyond making money.
2.  **Identify** your top three target audience personas by listing their specific age range, income bracket, and primary pain point for each.
3.  **Select** a primary color palette by choosing exactly one dominant color, two supporting colors, and one neutral (e.g., #2B59C3, #F4A261, #E9C46A, #F8F9FA) with hex codes.
4.  **Choose** two typefaces (one for headlines, one for body text) from a single font family or a complementary pairing, and document their exact names and weights (e.g., "Montserrat Bold" and "Open Sans Regular").
5.  **Write** three distinct brand voice descriptors (e.g., "Professional, Empathetic, Direct") and create one sample sentence for each to illustrate tone.
6.  **Create** a logo usage guide with three specific rules: minimum size (e.g., 1 inch wide), clear space requirement (e.g., 0.5 inch padding on all sides), and approved color variations (e.g., full color, black, white).
7.  **List** five key brand adjectives (e.g., "Innovative, Trustworthy, Bold, Minimal, Sustainable") and rank them by priority for consistency in all messaging.
8.  **Define** a photography or illustration style by specifying three concrete criteria (e.g., "natural lighting, candid subjects, warm filters" or "flat vector art, limited color palette, 2D perspective").
