Here are 5 specific templates for a **Brand Identity Template Pack**, each designed to help businesses, designers, or startups define, organize, and communicate their visual and strategic brand elements.

---

### 1. The Brand Strategy Foundation Template
**Purpose:** To define the core "why" behind the brand—its mission, vision, values, and personality.

**What it contains:**
- **Mission Statement** (3-line fillable box)
- **Vision Statement** (1-2 sentence future goal)
- **Core Values** (list of 3–5 values with space for a short explanation)
- **Brand Personality** (5 adjectives, e.g., "playful, trustworthy, bold")
- **Target Audience Persona** (name, age, job, pain points, goals)
- **Brand Voice & Tone** (e.g., "friendly but professional" / "humorous vs. serious")

**How to use it:**
- Fill it out during a brand workshop or solo brainstorming session.
- Keep this template as the "north star" for all design and copy decisions.
- Revisit quarterly to ensure consistency as the business evolves.

---

### 2. The Visual Identity Specification Sheet
**Purpose:** To document all visual elements (logo, colors, typography, imagery) in one place for designers and marketers.

**What it contains:**
- **Primary Logo** (space for 2–3 variations: full color, reversed, monochrome)
- **Color Palette** (hex, RGB, CMYK codes for primary, secondary, accent, and neutral colors)
- **Typography** (font names, weights, and usage rules for headings, body text, and buttons)
- **Iconography Style** (line vs. filled, rounded vs. sharp corners)
- **Imagery Guidelines** (photo style, illustration style, texture preferences)
