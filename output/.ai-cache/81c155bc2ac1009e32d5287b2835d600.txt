Here is a 10-item actionable checklist for creating a **Logo Design Inspiration Pack**. Each item is specific, measurable, and starts with a verb.

1. **Curate** a collection of 50 high-resolution logos (PNG/JPG) from 5 distinct industries (e.g., Tech, Food, Fashion, Health, Finance) by the end of the week.
2. **Organize** the logos into 5 digital folders labeled by design style (e.g., "Minimalist", "Mascot", "Geometric", "Hand-drawn", "Vintage").
3. **Extract** the primary color palette (3-5 hex codes) from 10 of your favorite logos using a color picker tool.
4. **Identify** and list the top 5 typography styles used in the collection (e.g., Sans-serif, Serif, Script, Slab, Display) with font name examples.
5. **Annotate** 10 logo examples with brief notes explaining why their negative space usage is effective.
6. **Create** a single-page mood board (digital or physical) featuring the 5 strongest logo concepts that represent "simplicity and scalability."
7. **Design** 3 quick thumbnail sketches for a new logo concept, directly inspired by the shapes and layouts found in the pack.
8. **Compile** a one-page PDF document listing 10 common logo pitfalls (e.g., complexity, poor kerning) with 2 examples from the pack for each.
9. **Write** a 50-word summary for 5 logos in the pack, explaining the metaphor or story behind the symbol.
10. **Export** the entire pack as a single, password-protected, compressed ZIP file under 50 MB, ready for client or team use.
