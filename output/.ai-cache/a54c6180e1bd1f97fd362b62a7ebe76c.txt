Here is a 10-item actionable checklist for a Font Pairing Guide, designed for practical implementation.

**Font Pairing Guide: Actionable Checklist**

1.  **Analyze** your brand’s core personality (e.g., modern, traditional, playful) and write down 3-5 adjectives before selecting any typeface.
2.  **Select** one primary "workhorse" font for body text (e.g., a highly readable serif or sans-serif) and test it at 16px on a mobile screen for 5 minutes.
3.  **Choose** one contrasting display font for headings (e.g., pair a geometric sans-serif with a humanist serif) and verify they differ in weight, structure, or x-height.
4.  **Test** the chosen pair in a real-world layout (headline + paragraph) and confirm the contrast is clear, not chaotic, by reading it aloud.
5.  **Limit** your project to a maximum of 2 fonts (one for body, one for headings) and only add a third for specific accents (e.g., quotes, buttons).
6.  **Verify** both fonts support the same language character set (e.g., Latin, Cyrillic) and include all necessary punctuation and special characters for your content.
7.  **Check** the x-height ratio between your body and heading fonts—ensure the body font’s x-height is at least 70% of the heading font’s x-height for visual harmony.
8.  **Preview** the pair in at least 3 different sizes (e.g., 12px, 24px, 48px) and confirm no letters become illegible or awkwardly spaced.
9.  **Load** both fonts on a live test page and measure the page load time—ensure the total font weight is under 300KB (or use system fonts as a fallback).
