Shopify Store Setup Guide - Complete Guide

1. Account Creation and Plan Selection

Start by signing up at Shopify.com with your email. Choose a plan that fits your budget and needs—Basic Shopify at $39/month is ideal for new stores, while Shopify at $105/month unlocks advanced reporting and lower transaction fees. Use the 14-day free trial to explore features before committing.

2. Theme Customization and Branding

Select a free or premium theme from the Shopify Theme Store—Dawn is a popular free option for speed and flexibility. Customize colors, fonts, and logo using the drag-and-drop editor. Add your brand voice to the homepage hero section with a clear value proposition and call-to-action button.

3. Product Listing and Inventory Setup

Add products manually or import via CSV. For each item, include high-resolution images, a compelling title, detailed description with bullet points, and SKU for inventory tracking. Set pricing, compare-at price for discounts, and shipping weight. Use Shopify’s inventory management to track stock levels across variants.

4. Payment Gateway and Shipping Configuration

Activate Shopify Payments to accept credit cards with 0% transaction fees on Basic plan (2.9% + 30¢ per transaction). Add PayPal, Apple Pay, and Google Pay for broader coverage. Configure shipping zones and rates—offer free shipping over $50 to boost average order value. Use carrier-calculated rates for accuracy.

5. Launch Checklist and Marketing Setup

Before going live, test checkout flow, mobile responsiveness, and page load speed (aim for under 3 seconds). Install essential apps: Oberlo for dropshipping, Loox for reviews, and Mailchimp for email marketing. Set up Google Analytics and Facebook Pixel to track traffic and conversions. Finally, remove password protection and announce your store on social media.
